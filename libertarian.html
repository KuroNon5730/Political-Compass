Libertarian Left

The Model of Social Liberty: Horizontal Authority, Collective Ownership, and Individual Autonomy.

I. Social Ownership & Mutualism

01

Believes natural resources and means of production should be held by the community.

02

Property rights are based on "Usufruct" (use and occupancy) rather than legal deeds.

03

Rejects private profit in favor of "Production for Use" to meet community needs.

04

Advocates for businesses to be owned and managed democratically by the workers.

05

Utilizes "Mutual Aid" as a voluntary reciprocal exchange outside of state or market.

06

Supports the abolition of wage labor in favor of equal economic partnerships.

07

Economic coordination occurs through local assemblies rather than a central state.

08

Views housing as a human right and actively opposes the concept of rent.

09

Promotes "Gift Economies" where goods are shared without explicit debt or trade.

10

Rejects intellectual property laws, believing knowledge belongs to the commons.

II. Horizontalism & Direct Democracy

11

Advocates for "Direct Democracy" where every individual participates in policy-making.

12

Utilizes "Consensus Decision-Making" to ensure all voices are heard and considered.

13

Rejects "Top-Down" leadership in favor of flat, horizontal network structures.

14

Shifts political power to the "Libertarian Municipalism" (neighborhood) level.

15

Delegates are "Recallable," meaning they can be removed instantly by their community.

16

Communities link together via "Federation" for large-scale coordination without a state.

17

Rejects the "Social Contract" as a tool used by states to enforce obedience.

18

Supports "Free Association," the right to join or leave any group at will.

19

Encourages "Dual Power" by building community institutions that rival state power.

20

Historical precedent: The Ukrainian Free Territory (Makhnovshchina) applied these rules.

III. Bodily Autonomy & Inclusivity

21

Asserts absolute "Bodily Autonomy" over reproductive and personal health choices.

22

Views social hierarchies (racism, sexism) as being linked to state and class power.

23

Actively seeks to dismantle "Patriarchy" and male-dominated social structures.

24

Supports the complete decriminalization of drugs and victimless personal acts.

25

Advocates for "Open Borders" and the free movement of all human beings.

26

Follows "Secularism," removing all religious authority from social governance.

27

Prioritizes "Intersectionality" in understanding how different oppressions overlap.

28

Values "Radical Inclusivity" as a method to protect marginalized identities.

29

Believes that personal freedom requires a society that provides for basic needs.

30

Education focuses on critical thinking rather than obedience to authority.

IV. Restorative Justice & Safety

31

Advocates for the "Abolition of Prisons" in favor of community-led rehabilitation.

32

Replaces state police with "Community Safety Initiatives" and local crisis teams.

33

Focuses on "Restorative Justice," where the goal is to heal the harm done to victims.

34

Rejects "Retributive Justice" (punishment) as a failed state-mandated tool.

35

Utilizes community mediation to solve local disputes without legal escalation.

36

Opposes "Imperialism" and all forms of state-led military aggression.

37

Advocates for global "Anti-War" movements as a core tenet of human liberty.

38

Believes that "Law and Order" is often a code for protecting the property of the elite.

39

Promotes "Prefigurative Politics"—living today as if the state were already gone.

40

Slogan: "No Gods, No Masters" expresses total rejection of political authority.

V. Social Ecology & Ideology

41

Follows "Social Ecology," believing environmental issues stem from social hierarchy.

42

Distinguishes between "Personal Property" (clothes) and "Private Property" (factories).

43

Advocates for "Anarcho-Communism," the synthesis of freedom and social equality.

44

Rejects the "Vanguard Party" model used by the Authoritarian Left.

45

Encourages "Class Consciousness" to help workers organize against hierarchies.

46

Promotes "Spontaneous Revolution" led by the people, not a state-like party.

47

Believes that "Power Corrupts," even when held by those with good intentions.

48

Values "Industrial Democracy," where unions act as the primary social organizers.

49

Advocates for the "Commons," resources managed shared by all for the benefit of all.

50

Synthesis: Combines individual liberty with the rejection of private accumulation.
