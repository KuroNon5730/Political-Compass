Libertarian Right

The Model of Market Liberty: Individual Sovereignty, Private Property, and Spontaneous Order.

I. Self-Ownership & The NAP

01

Foundational belief that every individual has absolute jurisdiction over their own body.

02

The Non-Aggression Principle (NAP) asserts that initiating force is always illegitimate.

03

Homesteading Principle: Ownership is gained by mixing labor with unowned resources.

04

Asserts that taxation is theft because it is taken without individual consent.

05

Voluntaryism: All forms of human association must be purely consensual.

06

Negative Liberty: Defined as the absence of obstacles or constraints by others.

07

Natural Rights: Belief that rights are inherent to humans, not granted by states.

08

Radical Responsibility: Individuals must bear the full consequences of their choices.

09

Property as Liberty: Belief that without property, no other rights can truly exist.

10

Rejects "Sovereign Immunity," holding government agents liable for rights violations.

II. Free Markets & Austrian Theory

11

Laissez-faire: Total absence of government intervention in economic affairs.

12

Subjective Theory of Value: Value is determined by individual preference, not labor.

13

Spontaneous Order: Complex markets organize themselves without central planning.

14

Economic Calculation Problem: Planning fails without market-driven price signals.

15

Sound Money: Advocacy for gold or crypto to prevent state-led inflation.

16

Creative Destruction: Inefficient firms must fail to allow better ones to emerge.

17

Consumer Sovereignty: Consumers dictate production through their spending choices.

18

Methodological Individualism: Analyzing society through the lens of individual action.

19

Praxeology: The study of human action based on logical axioms and goals.

20

Anti-Subsidy: Total opposition to corporate bailouts and "corporate welfare."

III. Privatization & Private Law

21

Advocates for the privatization of all public lands, roads, and services.

22

Polycentric Law: Competing private court systems instead of a state monopoly.

23

Contract Law: All social relations should be governed by voluntary agreements.

24

Private Defense Agencies (PDA): Security provided by private insurance/firms.

25

Abolition of the Federal Reserve: Returning currency control to the market.

26

Deregulation: Removal of all state licenses and barriers to business entry.

27

Free Trade: Total abolition of tariffs, quotas, and international trade barriers.

28

Intellectual Property Skepticism: Arguments that ideas cannot be physical property.

29

Right to Secede: Belief that any individual or community can leave a state at will.

30

Jury Nullification: The right of juries to strike down laws they deem unjust.

IV. Personal Liberty & Defense

31

Victimless Crimes: Drug use and gambling should be legal if no one is harmed.

32

Right to Bear Arms: Absolute support for ownership as a deterrent to tyranny.

33

Non-Interventionism: "Trade with all, alliances with none" foreign policy.

34

Anti-Conscription: Opposition to the draft as state-mandated slavery.

35

Right to Discriminate: Private owners may choose who to associate with on their land.

36

Freedom of Association: The right to join or leave any organization for any reason.

37

Abolition of Borders: Some argue borders are state-imposed limits on labor.

38

Anti-Social Engineering: States should not attempt to "shape" human behavior.

39

Charity over Welfare: Voluntary aid is seen as more moral and efficient than state aid.

40

Agorism: Using black/gray markets to peacefully bypass state control.

V. Minarchism to Anarcho-Capitalism

41

Minarchism: Belief in a "Night-Watchman State" limited to police, courts, and military.

42

Anarcho-Capitalism: Rejection of all state functions in favor of market services.

43

Paleolibertarianism: Combines market liberty with traditional social values.

44

Left-Libertarianism (Market): Uses Lib-Right economics to reach Lib-Left social ends.

45

Enlightened Self-Interest: Individual gain inadvertently helps society (The Invisible Hand).

46

Capital Accumulation: Reinvesting profit is the only way to raise living standards.

47

Decentralization: Pushing all power to the smallest local or individual level.

48

Price Signals: Prices are essential "information" for resource allocation.

49

Anti-Imperialism: Opposition to all state-led foreign wars and occupation.

50

The Sovereign Individual: The goal of becoming a self-governing entity.
