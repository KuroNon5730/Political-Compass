Anarcho-Communism

The Free Commune: Abolition of the State, Currency, and Private Property.

Abolition of Power (Facts 01-25)

01

Abolition of the State: The immediate destruction of all borders, taxes, and police forces.

02

Gift Economy: Distribution of goods based purely on need without currency or bartering.

03

Direct Action: Rejection of political parties in favor of occupations and mutual aid.

04

Consensus Democracy: Every member of a commune must reach a shared agreement for any action.

05

Work as Play: The transformation of labor into voluntary, creative activity.

Communal Life (Facts 26-30)

26

Common Ownership: The total lack of title deeds; the Earth belongs to no one.

27

Restorative Circles: Replacing prisons with community-led reconciliation meetings.

28

Prefigurative Politics: Living today as if the state has already been abolished.

29

Abolition of Property: Distinguishing between "personal" items and "private" capital.

30

Syndicalist Federations: Coordination through temporary associations of worker councils.

Communal Dynamics (Facts 31-40)

31

Mutual Aid: Voluntary resource exchange based on community solidarity.

32

Usufruct Rights: Property rights based solely on active usage and occupancy.

33

Affinity Groups: Temporary clusters formed for specific social or tactical tasks.

34

Platformism: Organizing anarchist groups with unified tactical and theoretical goals.

35

Free Access: Removing barriers to all community-held resources.

36

Recallable Delegates: Temporary reps removable by the commune at any moment.

37

Anti-Work: Rejection of wage-labor in favor of voluntary, creative fulfillment.

38

Community Self-Defense: Decentralized militias replacing the professional military.

39

Horizontalism: Refusal to allow permanent positions of authority or leadership.

40

Squatting: Reclaiming unused space as a legitimate social act.

Libertarian Socialism (Facts 41-50)

41

Conquest of Bread: Theory of post-state food distribution without money.

42

Revolutionary Catalonia: Historical precedent of worker-run industry without bosses.

43

Anarcho-Syndicalism: Using unions as the vehicle to build an anarchist society.

44

Equal Authority Education: Schools where students and teachers share governance.

45

The Black Bloc: Anonymous collective action against state/capitalist symbols.

46

Abolition of Borders: National boundaries viewed as violent artificial constructs.

47

Social Ecology: Belief that hierarchy is the root cause of environmental destruction.

48

General Strike: Stopping all labor to dismantle the state infrastructure.

49

Decentralized Planning: Local councils coordinating production without a center.

50

Anarchy is Order: Spontaneous social harmony arising once the state is removed.
